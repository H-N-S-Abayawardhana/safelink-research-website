1 Literature Survey

Existing research in public safety and community policing highlights limitations of manual, reactive systems for emergency handling, traffic monitoring, and harmful content moderation. Recent advances in AI, mobile systems, and multimodal analytics provide a foundation for proactive and context-aware safety intelligence.

Key areas reviewed include:

  • Crime hotspot prediction using spatio-temporal machine learning
  • Multilingual and code-mixed Sinhala-English NLP for harmful content detection
  • YOLO-based object detection for emergency and traffic surveillance
  • Multimodal emergency communication systems using video, audio, and GPS
  • License plate recognition pipelines for automated traffic enforcement

While these studies show strong individual outcomes, most solutions address isolated problems. There is still limited work on a unified, mobile-first platform tailored to Sri Lanka's social, linguistic, and infrastructure context.

2 Research Gap

Despite global progress in AI safety systems, implementation in Sri Lanka remains fragmented. Identified gaps include:

  • Lack of integration between physical-world monitoring, emergency response, and online threat detection
  • Limited bilingual systems that support Sinhala-English code-mixed real-world reports
  • Heavy dependence on manual monitoring for social media and emergency workflows
  • Insufficient predictive models for proactive intervention before incidents escalate

3 Research Problem

How can an AI-powered mobile platform improve public safety and community policing in Sri Lanka by enabling proactive risk prediction, real-time emergency support, harmful content moderation, and smart traffic analytics in one integrated framework?

The core challenge is to build a scalable, multilingual, and context-aware platform that supports both citizens and authorities with timely alerts, data-driven decisions, and improved situational awareness.

4 Research Objectives

  • Design a women and children safety risk prediction module using multimodal analytics
  • Implement an emergency SOS feature with real-time GPS, live streaming, and automated threat detection
  • Develop multilingual harmful social media content detection for proactive intervention
  • Build smart traffic violation detection and accident-risk prediction using computer vision
  • Deliver a unified mobile and dashboard ecosystem for preventive policing and response coordination

Methodology

Our research follows a structured, iterative approach to ensure reliable and validated results.

1

Multisource Data Collection

Collect incident media, emergency records, social media content, and traffic data relevant to Sri Lankan public safety.

2

Data Preprocessing

Normalize video, clean audio, and standardize Sinhala-English code-mixed text for reliable model training.

3

Model Development

Train YOLO, XLM-RoBERTa, and YAMNet based models for threat detection, classification, and risk estimation.

4

System Integration

Integrate all AI modules into a mobile application with secure backend services and police/admin dashboards.

5

Testing & Validation

Evaluate model precision, recall, F1-score, and mAP; then validate functional performance under real-time scenarios.

6

Real-Time Evaluation

Assess emergency stream reliability, location tracking, multimodal scoring, and dashboard responsiveness.

7

Optimization

Improve threshold tuning, false positive reduction, and multilingual robustness for deployment readiness.

8

Documentation

Publish technical reports, research findings, and implementation guidance for future national-scale adoption.
